# About Parallel and Perspective Views

You can create realistic visual effects in a drawing by defining either parallel or perspective projections of a model.

The difference between perspective views and parallel projections is that perspective views require a distance between a
theoretical camera and target point. Small distances produce severe perspective effects; large distances produce mild effects.

A perspective view remains in effect until the perspective effect is turned off or until a new view is defined in its place.
