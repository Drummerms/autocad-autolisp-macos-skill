# The Hitchhiker's Guide to AutoCAD for Mac

If you're new to AutoCAD for Mac or AutoCAD LT for Mac, this guide introduces you to the essential commands that you need
to create 2D drawings. It's also a great place to refresh your memory if you just completed your initial training or if you
use AutoCAD for Mac only occasionally.

![](../images/GUID-6CE3BE32-D9C2-442D-B70E-178FBE09827D-low.png)

1. [Basics](Basics.md)

   Review the basic AutoCAD for Mac controls.
2. [View](View.md)

   Pan and zoom to different views in a drawing.
3. [Geometry](Geometry.md)

   Create basic geometric objects such as lines, circles, and hatched areas.
4. [Precision](Precision.md)

   Ensure the precision required for your models.
5. [Layers](Layers.md)

   Organize your drawing by assigning objects to layers.
6. [Properties](Properties.md)

   Assign properties such as color and linetype to individual objects, or as default properties assigned to layers.
7. [Modify](Modify.md)

   Perform operations such as erase, move, and trim on the objects in a drawing.
8. [Symbols](Symbols.md)

   Insert symbols and details into your drawings from commercial online sources or from your own designs.
9. [Layout](Layout.md)

   Display one or more scaled views of your design on a standard-size drawing sheet called a layout.
10. [Annotate](Annotate.md)

    Create notes, labels, bubbles, and callouts. Save and restore style settings by name.
11. [Dimension](Dimension.md)

    Create several types of dimensions and save dimension settings by name.
12. [Print](Print.md)

    Output a drawing layout to a printer, a plotter, or a file. Save and restore the printer settings for each layout.
