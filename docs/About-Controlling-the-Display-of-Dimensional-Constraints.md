# About Controlling the Display of Dimensional Constraints

You can display or hide dynamic and annotational constraints within a drawing.

### Display or Hide Dynamic Constraints

You can hide all dynamic constraints to reduce clutter when you want to work with geometric constraints only, or when you
need to continue other work in the drawing. You can turn on their display with the DCDISPLAY command.

By default, if you select an object associated with a hidden dynamic constraint, all dynamic constraints associated with that
object are temporarily displayed.

You can display or hide the dynamic constraints for all objects or for a selection set.

## Display or Hide Annotational Constraints

You control the display of annotational constraints as you would with dimension objects—you assign them to a layer and turn
the layer on or off as needed. You can also specify object properties for annotational constraints such as dimension style,
color, and lineweight.
